Research VR Experience

Dolly's World

An escape-room-style VR game exploring queer relationships, labor, and class through surreal children’s television aesthetics.

Dolly’s World is an escape-room-style virtual reality game developed in Unity for the Oculus Quest by undergraduate researchers in the Computer Science and English Departments under the mentorship of Assistant Professor of Computer Science, Dr. Sarah Schoemann.

In Dolly’s World, players enter the surreal setting of a fictional 1990s/early 2000s children’s television show inspired by the strange and kitschy aesthetics of programs such as Pee-Wee’s Playhouse. As players solve environmental puzzles, they uncover the story of Bee, a recent college graduate in a queer romantic relationship with her coworker, Jess. Through dialogue and exploration, players learn about class tensions within their relationship, unstable labor conditions at the fast-casual restaurant Krust Krafters, and suspicions of wage theft by their exploitative manager.

Although video games have made strides toward more diverse representation in recent years, queer-centered narratives remain uncommon, particularly in VR titles. At the same time, rising income inequality and labor movements such as the Fight for $15 have renewed attention to worker rights in the service industry. Through sharing the story of Bee and Jess, Dolly’s World contributes to the growing body of independent games that use interactive media as a space for exploring themes of identity and class which reflect real world issues.

My Role on Dolly's World
  • Owned implementation of interactive gameplay systems and puzzle logic in Unity for Oculus Quest.
  • Collaborated with writers and researchers to translate narrative beats into concrete interactions and level flows.
  • Iterated on environmental interactions, player guidance, and usability in VR from playtest feedback.
  • Supported debugging, profiling, and deployment builds for research demonstrations and internal milestones.
